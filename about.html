Our Mission

"We build digital tools that facilitate the delivery of care and empower healthcare recipients to achieve better outcomes."

Data can be useful if accurate but questionable, if not harmful, when inaccurate. By improving the management of health data, we will be able to significantly improve the quality of care received by a vast population of citizens, improve the lives of the talented practitioners who already achieve so much with limited tools and empower decision makers to act in a timely manner with quality data at their disposal.

“Without good data, we’re flying blind. If you can’t see it, you can’t solve it.”

Kofi Annan, Former Secretary-General of the United Nations


Globally, healthcare is undergoing technological reform. The emergence of new technologies (machine learning, blockchain) makes continued innovation inevitable. In more commercially competitive economies, we see information technology enhance diagnostic capabilities, data management, population health monitoring and more.

Throughout the nations of Africa, we are also seeing an increase in the adoption of technology to automate processes in many sectors. However, healthcare has unfortunately not enjoyed the same level of attention and investment until now.

Sub-Saharan Africa has been the most disease burdened region for over 3 decades along with being one of the most medically underserved regions globally. Per the world bank’s data, the doctor-to-population ratio is 1000. Additionally, the region also ranks lowest, collectively, on the world scale on 2 key health equity measuring indexes (the HAQ index and the WHO’s composite index).

As the global community reacts to the current COVID19 pandemic, these underdeveloped healthcare infrastructures are laid bare. There is a cultural and social transformation taking place and we are determined to play a leading role in this technological revolution within the healthcare domain and our region of focus, primarily.

Founder’s Story


This story set in motion some 40 years ago when Lorna Kingston, later followed by her younger sister Shirley Assandey, moved to the remote village of Bécédi, in the South-East of Côte d'Ivoire to support the care of at-risk pre/postnatal patients.

These two veteran public health workers are the aunt and mother, respectively, of the founder of La Ruche Health, Rory Assandey.

Shirley Assandey

Trainee Nurse, Belfast Ireland (1980 - 1985)

Geriatic Nurse, Cork Ireland (2002 - 2019).

FOUGHT MEASLES, YELLOW FEVER & POLIO

From the 80’s to the early 2000s, Shirley drove the execution of immunization programs against poliomyelitis, diphtheria, tetanus, pertussis, tuberculosis, yellow fever, and measles with considerable short and long term success.

REDUCED INFANT MORTALITY WITH PREVENTATIVE CARE

In 1987, ½ of all deaths in Côte d'Ivoire were children under the age of five. Shirley and her elder sister Lorna Kingston, along with their team, lead a Maternal Health Care (MHC) center that contributed significantly to the betterment of these conditions by transferring knowledge of effective healthcare education and delivery.

PREPAREDNESS FOR OUTBREAKS

Healthcare education, immunization programs and improvements in clinical processes and sanitary standards were all building blocks that lead to the country’s preparedness for outbreaks such as Ebola in 2014, Dengue fever in 2017 and most recently COVID19 in 2020 being judged a success by the global health community


Having had a front row seat to the above successes, our founder remained a huge fan of health workers, the “silent heroes”. While he did not gravitate into the field directly, Rory Assandey intentionally studied Information Technology as a science and realized a successful career in enterprise IT consulting at SAP, a multinational world leader in enterprise IT solutions and implementations.

In this next chapter, Rory leads a team of social impact entrepreneurs united by a shared passion to provide health equity to health workers and the communities they serve. 100% of our core team lives or has lived in Sub-Saharan Africa and have been directly impacted with devastating effect by the inefficiencies of the health systems currently in place. This has served as a motivation for us to pursue our mission with diligence and focus.
