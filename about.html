About
La Ruche Health

This story was set in motion some 40 years ago when Lorna Kingston, later followed by her younger sister Shirley Assandey, moved to the remote village of Bécédi, in the South-East of Côte d'Ivoire to support the care of at-risk prenatal (before birth) and postpartum patients.

These two veteran health workers are the aunt and mother, respectively, of La Ruche Health founder, Rory Assandey.

Our Purpose

Digitalise

We want to strengthen the management of health data, starting with primary care, across the health network. It is evident that poor data management is linked to medical errors, high mortality rates, and deficient population health monitoring. We are motivated to play a leading role in the construction of fundamental technology building blocks that drive digital health across the global south.

Connect

We want to increase awareness and access to fundamental health services. We observe a lack of awareness & access to such services which is linked to failed preventative care, avoidable deaths, and poverty. By introducing a freely available patient portal and incentivizing patients to utilize it for an improved experience when they come into contact with the healthcare system, it can be leveraged to increase their awareness of key health services such as health financing schemes, pharmacy services and health education resources. We are also seeking out partnerships with other service providers, who align with our goals, to load the patient portal with additional health products & services.

Standardize

We see significant losses of revenue particularly within the public health network linked to fraud in the billing, pharmaceutical supply chain, and the insurance claims process. We want to introduce transparency and accountability here so that facilities can combat the loss in revenue, which can then be reinvested into critical resources such as staff, training, beds, medical equipment, IT infrastructure etc.

Standardize

In focusing on the above objectives, we believe that we will increase performance rankings when it comes to public health performance and equity within the regions we serve. We track these using ranking methodologies that include:
efficiency on the composite index
performance on the HAQ index

Reduced Infant Mortality With Preventative Care

In 1987, ½ of all deaths in Côte d'Ivoire were children under the age of five. Shirley and her elder sister Lorna Kingston, along with their team, lead a Maternal Health Care (MHC) center that contributed significantly to the betterment of these conditions by transferring knowledge of effective healthcare education and delivery.

Fought Measles, Yellow Fever & Polio

From the 80’s to the early 2000s, this team of health care workers at ‘La martenite et dispensaire de becedi’, a centre owned by the order, “Sœurs de la charité de Notre-Dame d'Évron” drove the execution of immunization programs against poliomyelitis, diphtheria, tetanus, pertussis, tuberculosis, yellow fever, and measles with considerable short and long term success.

Preparedness For Outbreaks

Healthcare education, immunization programs and improvements in clinical processes and sanitary standards were all building blocks that led to the country’s preparedness for outbreaks such as Ebola in 2014, Dengue fever in 2017 and most recently COVID19 in 2020 being judged a success by the global health community.

Next Chapter

In this next chapter, Rory Assandey leads a team of social impact entrepreneurs united by a passion to help champion health workers and the communities they serve. Our team has been directly impacted by the healthcare challenges present in low to middle-income countries and therefore possess a shared motivation to pursue our mission with diligence and focus.
